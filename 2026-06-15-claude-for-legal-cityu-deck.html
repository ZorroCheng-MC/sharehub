Introduction

What is Claude for Legal?

A free, open collection of ready-made AI assistants for the legal workflows lawyers do every day — installed into Claude.

🧩

Not one bot — many

13 practice-area plugins (commercial, corporate, privacy, litigation, IP, employment…) plus law-school tools. Install only what you need.

📋

Workflow-shaped

Each knows a real task end-to-end — "review this vendor contract", "draft this DSAR response" — with cited sources and house formatting.

🔒

Built by Anthropic

Reference tooling, free and open. Every output is a draft for attorney review — designed to make review faster, not to replace the lawyer.

Source: Anthropic claude-for-legal marketplace (commercial · privacy · product · corporate · employment · litigation · regulatory · AI-governance · IP · law-student · legal-clinic · builder-hub · CoCounsel).

Section 2
How It Works

The architecture that makes it adapt to you — and to Hong Kong

The Core Idea

It learns your way of working — once

STEP 1
Cold-start interview
It asks about your jurisdiction, risk posture, approvers and house style.
STEP 2
Builds a profile
Your answers are saved to a "practice profile" file.
STEP 3
Every skill reads it
All plugins now work the way you described — no re-explaining.
STEP 4
/customize
Change one thing — add a jurisdiction — without redoing setup.

Every plugin ships a cold-start-interview and a customize skill. This is where you set "Hong Kong" or "PRC" — see Section 5.

One Source of Truth

The practice profile

Two plain-text files store everything the plugins know about your practice. Edit them, and every skill changes behaviour.

🏢

company-profile.md

Shared across all 13 plugins — who you are, your industry, your jurisdictions. Change it once → flows everywhere.

HK lever: set jurisdiction here to make the whole suite Hong-Kong-aware.
📑

each plugin's CLAUDE.md

Per-practice specifics — your contract playbook, escalation contacts, review rules, materiality thresholds.

Example: "escalate any indemnity cap above HK$5M to the GC."

Stored under ~/.claude/plugins/config/claude-for-legal/. No coding — they're readable text files.

Vocabulary

Four building blocks

⚡

Skills

The tasks you trigger by name — /review, /dsar-response. The verbs.

🧑‍⚖️

Named Agents

Job-titled end-to-end workers: "Vendor Agreement Reviewer", "DSAR Responder".

⏰

Watchers

Scheduled agents that run on their own — renewal, docket, reg-feed — and ping you.

🔌

Connectors

Links to your systems — Slack, Drive, Box, iManage, DocuSign, CourtListener.

A real workflow usually chains them: a Watcher spots a renewal → triggers a Skill → which reads a Connector → and a named Agent drafts the memo.

End-to-End

Worked example: inbound NDA

ARRIVES
NDA lands
Counterparty sends an NDA to the shared inbox / connector.
TRIAGE
GREEN / YELLOW / RED
Checked against your playbook; flagged by severity.
DRAFT
Redline + memo
Deviations marked, with a plain-English summary.
GATE
Lawyer reviews
Only the hard ones reach a desk. Human signs off.
The win: routine "greens" clear themselves. Lawyer time goes only where judgement is actually needed.

Section 5
Hong Kong & China

Adapting a US-built suite for HK common law and PRC civil law

Set Expectations First

Scaffolding, not a local law database

🇺🇸

What ships inside

US/EU defaults: FMLA & US state worker tests, GDPR DPIAs, ABA Op. 512, Westlaw / CourtListener connectors.

🔧

Where HK/China value is

The workflow scaffolding + the customisation layer. You supply local law via the practice profile and the sources you feed it.

Be upfront: the plugins are jurisdiction-agnostic structure. The HK/PRC content comes from you. That honesty is what earns a scholarly room's trust.
Sort It Out

Transfers cleanly vs needs remapping

✅

Transfers well (HK common law)

  • Litigation workflow & work product
  • Contract / NDA review structure
  • IP triage, diligence grids, drafting
⚠️

Needs real remapping

  • US-statute logic: FMLA → Employment Ordinance; GDPR → PDPO
  • US worker tests → HK contract-of-service tests
  • Precedent-driven skills under PRC civil law (no binding precedent)

Must-Teach

Data residency & confidentiality

The moment client or personal data enters a cloud LLM, PRC data-export rules and HK PDPO obligations are engaged. Teach the limits.

🚫

The risk

  • PRC cross-border data-export restrictions
  • HK PDPO duties on personal data
  • Privilege & client confidentiality
✅

Safe patterns to teach

  • Redact identifiers before prompting
  • Work on de-identified / abstract facts
  • Know where the model runs & what's retained

This is a legal-ethics teaching topic in its own right — and the first question your audience will ask.

Ethics Layer

Professional conduct remap

The suite encodes US ethics (ABA Op. 512). For HK, map to the Solicitors' Guide & the Bar Code of Conduct — the three governing duties are universal:

  • Competence — understand what the tool can and can't do, and its jurisdiction limits.
  • Confidentiality — the data-residency discipline from the previous slide.
  • Supervision — a qualified lawyer reviews and owns every output. The gate is non-negotiable.

A natural CPD module — and a recurring theme through every plugin's design.

Remember Five Things

Key takeaways

  • It's 13 workflow assistants, not one robot lawyer — install what you need.
  • Every output is a draft for attorney review — cited, conservative, gated.
  • It learns your practice once (the profile) and every skill reuses it.
