Audience: Master Concept team — Marketing, Sales, Web/Dev, Curriculum, Product

Revision focus: `智啟學教` is treated as existing funded demand, not a news hook. The new mechanism is the School Efficiency Grant, and the growth play is to surface existing K12 courses, sell platform/services, and build a managed AI Lead offer.

Bottom line

  1. Training — now, recurring. Anchor on the Blueprint's 30-hour-per-3-year teacher AI training mandate.
  2. Platform / services — structural. Use the School Efficiency Grant to sell Gemini Enterprise, Claude, and LLM services.
  3. Managed AI Lead service — build this. Position MC as the alternative to hiring one digital-transformation person.

The three plays

Play 1 — Training on the 30-hour mandate

  • Frame K12 courses as the turnkey, compliant way to meet the 30-hour teacher AI training requirement.
  • Differentiate from free EDB training with tool-specific depth, hands-on labs, school-customised cohorts, and HK-based 繁中 delivery.
  • Anchor on Google Cloud Skills Boost certificates and Premier Partner credibility.

Play 2 — Platform / services on the School Efficiency Grant

  • Schools may spend the grant on AI solutions, LLM services, platforms and software.
  • Timeline is slower: apply by 31 Aug, funds from November, with a freeze-of-posts mechanism.
  • Build this as a 2027/28 pipeline.

Play 3 — Managed AI Lead service

  • The grant's first-named use is hiring a digital-transformation staffer.
  • Product concept: a managed / embedded AI Lead for Schools.
  • Validate procurement rules before pitching it as a grant-eligible service.

Positioning / messaging

  • Training: “Meet your teachers' 30-hour requirement with hands-on, tool-specific courses.”
  • Platform: “Spend your School Efficiency Grant on the AI tools your school will actually use.”
  • Managed service: “Don't gamble on hiring an AI person. Get a managed AI Lead and give your whole school capability.”
  • Use EDB language: 減負增能, 智慧校園, AI+課程, 智能向善.

K12 courses — surface & map

  • Audit the K12 courses and map each to mandate hours, funding source, and Blueprint priority.
  • Identify only true gaps.
  • Produce a one-page “K12 course → mandate hours → funding source” map for Sales.

Website

  • Promote K12 out of the filter into the nav.
  • Add a funding context block to the K12 view.
  • Add the mandate framing: label courses that count toward the 30-hour requirement.
  • Add the managed AI Lead service as an offering on the schools view.
  • Build a light /schools landing page.

Sales playbook

  1. Training: “Your teachers owe 30 hours of AI training every 3 years.”
  2. Harvest `智啟學教`: “You already received HK$500k under `智啟學教`.”
  3. Platform / managed: “For next year's School Efficiency Grant, you can buy AI tools — or take our managed AI Lead instead.”
  4. De-risk: compliant, itemised quoting and grant-vote materials.

Marketing plan

  • Principal webinar: “Meeting the 30-hour AI training mandate — and the smart alternative to hiring an AI person.”
  • Lead magnet: K12 catalogue mapped to mandate + funding.
  • Outreach: email + LinkedIn to principals and digital-ed coordinators.
  • Lighthouse school: sign one fast, publish a case study.
  • SEO keywords: 教師 30 小時 AI 培訓, 學校效率津貼, 學校 AI 培訓, 數字教育藍圖, 智啟學教.
